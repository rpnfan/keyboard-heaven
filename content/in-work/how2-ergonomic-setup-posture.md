Get the keyboard close to your body - very close. And use a symmetrical hand position. That means typing the c-key with the index, x with the middle finger and so on. The top left is still shifted to the left, but you cannot optimize that on a standard keyboard. Switching to a split columnar stagger keyboard would allow to type completely symmetric. That is recommended. But even on a standard keyboard you can type ergonomic "enough" not to get any problems. Ah, typing with floating hand in that position. If that is too tiring the keyboard is too far away still! The sides of the body support the arms when you have the keyboard close.
